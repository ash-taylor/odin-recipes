Maple & Pomegranate Duck, Spiced Lentils & Dukkah

Description

Rich and juicy British duck breast is the perfect canvas for a sticky-sweet drizzle of maple syrup and pomegranate molasses.
You dish it up with spiced stewed lentils, full of fibre. Grilled seasonal vegetables and a dusting of nutty dukkah to finish it off.
A feast for the eyes - and your bellies.

Ingredients

  • 2 duck breasts
  • 2 tbsp dukkah (Celery, Nuts, Peanuts, Sesame)
  • 2 garlic clove
  • 80g red split lentils
  • 2 tsp maple syrup
  • 1 tbsp Mindful Chef Middle Eastern spice mix
  • 1 red onion
  • 1 handful of fresh flat-leaf parsley
  • 200g passata
  • 1 red pepper
  • 1 tbsp pomegranate molasses
  • 1 chicken stock cube (Celery)
  • 1 courgette

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 240C / fan 220C / gas mark 8. Boil a kettle. Heat a medium frying pan with 1/2 tbsp oil on medium heat.
    Thinly slice the onion. Add the onion and cook for 5 mins, until softening. Meanwhile, finely chop or crush the garlic.
    Dissolve the stock cube in a jug with 400ml boiling water.
  2. Add the garlic, cook for 1 min, then add the lentils, spice mix, stock and passata. Stir and cover with a lid.
    Simmer for 18-20 mins, until the lentils are cooked through. Add a splash of water if it gets too dry.
    Season with sea salt and black pepper.
  3. Cut a criss-cross pattern into the duck skin, careful not to cut through the flesh. Heat a medium frying pan on medium-high heat.
    Add the duck, skin-side down, and cook for 4 mins, until crispy. Season with sea salt.
    Transfer to a lined baking tray, skin-side up, and bake 8-10 mins, until cooked through.
    Remove and leave to rest before thinly slicing against the grain.
  4. Thinly slice the pepper. Slice the courgette into 1/2cm thick discs. Place on a lined baking tray and toss with 1/2 tbsp oil,
    a pinch of sea salt and black pepper. Roast for 13-15 mins, until soft and golden.
  5. Finely chop the parsley and stir it through the lentils. Make the glaze; place the maple syrup and molasses in a bowl and stir.
  6. Serve the lentils topped with the roasted veg and duck. Drizzle the duck with the glaze and sprinkle with the dukkah.
